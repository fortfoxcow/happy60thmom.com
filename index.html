Happy 60th Birthday, Mom!

For your 60th birthday, I've gathered 60 memories from me, Annie, Melanie, and Dad. I hope they are a good reminder of how much we love you and how important you have been in shaping our lives. I hope you enjoy reading through these memories!

Love,
Kim




From Annie

1 You were always sewing clothes and other things. I remember the fabric from almost every project that you made. Recently you gave me some purple and white striped stretchy fabric, and I recognized it as the fabric in a maternity dress that you made when you were pregnant with Kimberly.
2 Although at the time you didn't even really use computers much, you could still assemble computers from the parts you would pick up at supply stores throughout LA and Orange County. Those computers looked so hard to put together. I was really impressed that you could do this.
3 I still remember the melody of the chorus from that song that was popular as a result of that Chinese soap opera "Hai Oh" (I can't write the proper pin yin, but the title meant 'seagull'). You really, really liked that show, and cried while watching the last episode. A man and a woman, lovers, ran toward each other on a beach, but were shot by Communist soldiers before they could share a final embrace. It was pretty sad. I cried too.
4 You took me everywhere when I was a kid, and when Kimberly came along, you continued to do the same. Spending all that time during my early years in Taiwan definitely had an impact on who I am today.
5 You would wake me up and take me to debate tournaments, which usually started painfully early in the morning, and you would come back at night to pick me up, even when the tournaments ran late (and they often did). When I was upset that I didn't win, you wouldn't let me whine or feel sorry for myself. I probably didn't always appreciated at the time, but I think this made me a stronger person.
6 I paid attention when you were negotiating with vendors, customers, suppliers and other business associates during the time when you ran Kingtech. You were really, really good. People really like to you, and yet you also managed to get what you wanted. I try to keep that in mind when I negotiate with others in my day-to-day work.
7 You've always made the extra effort to make us almost anything that we would want to eat, even if it was a very specific, non-Chinese food request, like strawberry cake for my birthday, or a catering request like a whole ton of chow mein noodles for International Day.

From Kim

25

One time I walked home from Glenknoll because I wanted to. When I came home, you asked why I walked home. I lied and said my school didn’t let me use the phone to call home. To my surprise, you stuck up for me, drove to the school, and demanded to know why they didn’t let me call home!

Today, I am admitting that I lied. I’m sorry!
26 The only reason why the Creative Impulse magazines were ever printed was because of you. You drove me to the printers, to the publishing offices, to wherever I needed to go. You taught me how to hold other people accountable, how to be organized, and how to be responsible. These were important business lessons.
27 In senior year of high school, you would pick me up in the middle of the day and drive me home. Frequently, I would take a nap. You'd wake me up and drive me back to school. I remember these afternoons fondly.
28 You introduced me to coffee! Those packets of sweet caffeinated goodness. It was a wonderful moment in my life.
29 One time at Thanksgiving, you put ice cubes in your red wine! Who does that!
30 When I was young and had an eye cyst, you made me drink this bitter soup. Oddly, there was another soup made of pig’s snout that I really enjoyed. Probably because of the melted sugar rock crystals.
31 Lucky would sleep on the bottom step, and you'd get frustrated that that spot turned darker.
32 I love your chow mein, but I hate mushrooms. I was always touched when you made this dish without mushrooms because everyone else preferred it with mushrooms.
33 Your dedication in working with Melanie and her schoolwork was always inspiring and touching. You always tried to make sure that she did her best and was given the right opportunities.
34 I was always in between activities. You would always bring my lunch or dinner in the car. My favorite dish was fried rice because there were peas. Yum, my favorite! I also loved ketchup fried rice.
35 I always ate a ton of plain rice and fried rice. Your nickname for me was rice tank and water tank.
36 One night, I took scissors and cut holes into my Little Mermaid blanket. I think I did it out of curiosity, but regardless of the reason, I knew I would be in trouble. Surprisingly, you were not *that* mad. The next day, we went to Tall Mouse and purchased white patches for the holes.
37 I’d always complain about eating too much or being super full that I’d “bao za” (explode), and you’d always be quick to point out I was still standing in the pantry or holding a snack in my hand.
38 One of the best habits you instilled in me was eating fruit everyday. Rather than American dessert, we had fruit every night, after dinner, before bed. Melanie always liked it because it meant she could stay up later.
39 You always remember everyone's food preferences! Even if I commented once - I don't like this restaurant, you would remember. For example, I never liked Hong Kong plaza food court, or that one restaurant near Life Plaza because it served shark.
40 I remember the little brown couch that you purchased for Annie. You told me that you always wanted your kids to have the best and cutest things to play with, like little furniture.
41 When you got dentures, I laughed really hard. I know it's mean because it’s hard and embarrassing for you, but sometimes it’s easier to laugh than to recognize how difficult the situation is.
42 I am constantly amazed at how tech-savvy you are. The other day you posted a video with music and a picture collage with nice borders. You also check in at restaurants on Facebook and use Groupons and Living Social coupons. You shop online more than I do. You are much better with technology than many other parents I know.
43 Just the other night, you wrote this amazing text in our group family chat that made me laugh really hard: “This group text is concluded now. No more message, but Happy Moon Festival.”
44 I’m not sure why I enjoyed this so much, but whenever you were doing something else, I’d get your attention by repeating your name over and over and over. “Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom. Mom.”
45 You spend so much time taking care of your nieces and nephews, as if they were your own kids. This is especially true for those that came to live in California, like Jacqueline, Tina, and Fanting. I appreciate it because it makes our family seem bigger and our family activities more festive. Your generosity shows and makes every event special and memorable.
46 Yum, some of my favorite dishes: egg and tomato, dan bing, clear noodles with squash, string beans, fried rice, and anything with peas!
47 One time we went to an outlet very far away. You let me buy a ton of new clothes from ESPIRIT, including a blue jacket, green t-shirt, and jeans. I was incredibly excited to wear them. I wore them to school the next day and received compliments from Melissa, Connie, and my other friends.
48 On the way home from high school one day, I needed to throw up in the car because I contracted the flu. Before I made a mess, you gave me a tin can that was perfect for what I was just about to do. How were you always so prepared?
49 Even when you had to leave me at home, I always had the best food to eat. My favorite were those chicken breast sandwiches with sesame buns from Costco. It made me feel older because I was “making” my own food -- opening the plastic and placing it in the microwave for one minute and thirty seconds.
50 We used to play a game when I was young - you would hold out the palm of your hand, and I would place the tip of my finger in your palm. Then I’d try to escape your grip when you closed your hand!
51 Frequently, you would be the one doing the hard work of waking up, waking me up, remembering all of our activities, etc. You would let me sleep in the car while you drove me from place to place.
52 I remember you were crying when my grandfather (your dad) passed away. I felt terrible that I wasn’t able to go to his funeral, but I was happy that I could play a song for him during the er-hu portion of my senior recital.
53 Once I complained about Chinese School. Then next year, you unenrolled me. The truth is I really didn’t enjoy it, but now I wish I could read and write Chinese. Regardless, this was such an important lesson. Why pursue activities if you’re not enjoying them? You should never do things just for the sake of doing them. That was such a valuable lesson. It has really shaped who I am today.
54 Looking back at all the activities I was allowed to do, it’s clear that I was incredibly lucky. I learned very good lessons about managing my time, pursuing activities that I enjoyed, and being honest with myself.
55 You are like a lioness - forever strong in your beliefs and in your actions. There are a few times when I remember seeing your vulnerable side, usually when one of us, one of your friends, or one of your other family members hurt you. It’s always hard to see you sad, but it always made me feel closer to you. It’s a good reminder that while it’s important to be strong, it’s okay to hurt sometimes. It's only human!
